Dietary Recommendations

Focus on balanced meals with complex carbohydrates, lean protein, and healthy fats.

  • Berbuka (Breaking the Fast): Start with dates and water. Include a balanced meal with protein (grilled chicken, fish, beans), complex carbohydrates (whole grains, brown rice), and plenty of vegetables. Avoid fried and sugary foods.
  • Sahur (Pre-Dawn Meal): Choose slow-digesting foods like oats, whole-wheat bread, yogurt, and nuts to provide sustained energy throughout the day. Drink plenty of water.
  • Snacks (between Berbuka and Sahur): If needed, choose healthy snacks like fruits, vegetables with hummus, or a small handful of nuts.

Blood Glucose Monitoring

Monitor your blood glucose levels more frequently during Ramadan, especially:

  • Before Berbuka
  • 2 hours after Berbuka
  • Before Sahur
  • Mid-day
  • Any time you feel symptoms of hypoglycemia or hyperglycemia

Consult your healthcare provider for personalized blood glucose targets and action plans.

Hypoglycemia Management

Recognize the symptoms of hypoglycemia (low blood sugar): shakiness, sweating, dizziness, confusion, rapid heartbeat.

If you experience hypoglycemia:

  • Check your blood glucose level.
  • If your blood glucose is below 70 mg/dL (3.9 mmol/L), consume 15-20 grams of fast-acting carbohydrates (e.g., glucose tablets, fruit juice, regular soda).
  • Recheck your blood glucose level after 15 minutes.
  • If your blood glucose is still low, repeat the treatment.
  • Once your blood glucose is back to normal, have a small snack with protein and complex carbohydrates.

If you experience severe hypoglycemia or are unable to treat yourself, seek immediate medical attention.

Exercise During Ramadan

Maintain a moderate level of physical activity during Ramadan.

  • Avoid strenuous exercise during fasting hours.
  • Engage in light activities like walking or stretching.
  • If you exercise, monitor your blood glucose levels closely before, during, and after exercise.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water during non-fasting hours.

Hydration

Dehydration can significantly impact blood glucose levels. Ensure adequate hydration during non-fasting hours.

  • Drink plenty of water between Berbuka and Sahur.
  • Avoid sugary drinks, which can lead to rapid blood glucose spikes.
  • Consider incorporating hydrating foods like fruits and vegetables into your meals.
